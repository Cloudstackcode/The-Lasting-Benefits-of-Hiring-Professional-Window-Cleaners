As professional window cleaners, we don’t just make your windows look better—we protect them from long-term harm. We take the time to inspect your frames, screens, and sills to catch early signs of damage, water infiltration, or insect activity. It’s part of how we help our clients extend the life of their windows and avoid expensive repairs.

Plus, clean windows let in more natural light. That simple change can brighten rooms, improve mood, and even reduce energy costs by limiting the need for artificial lighting. It’s one of those small touches that makes a noticeable difference in how your home feels day to day.

Tips for Maintaining Your Windows Between Cleanings

We always encourage our clients to take care of their windows between professional cleanings. Here are a few simple tips that can go a long way:

  • Wipe down window sills regularly to prevent dust and moisture buildup.
  • Use a microfiber cloth and distilled water to spot-clean smudges.
  • Avoid ammonia-based products, which can damage tint and seals.
  • Keep nearby landscaping trimmed to reduce pollen and sap on glass.
  • Open windows periodically to promote airflow and prevent condensation.

Of course, no DIY approach will replace the deep clean we provide—but these steps help keep your windows looking great between visits.
